What Goalsreach Actually Does

Goalsreach is a self-accountability system for people who want fewer excuses and more honest wins. It ties your goals, habits, and “fun” into one structure – you earn points by doing the work, and you spend them on the things you’d usually do without thinking.

Core loop

Turn effort into a currency you care about

Every meaningful action – training, deep work, studying – earns accountability points. Those points become the gate for your indulgences: gaming, nights out, takeaways, whatever actually tempts you.

Instead of trying to “resist” distractions, Goalsreach makes indulgence the reward for showing up. No points, no reward.

Momentum

Built for streaks, not short motivation spikes

Goalsreach tracks streaks, point history, and weekly output in a way that feels calm instead of noisy. You see your trajectory, not just a checklist.

The UI leans on minimal colour, clear typography, and small motion to keep you focused on the work – not the app itself.

Direction

From solo discipline to shared accountability

The current release focuses on personal accountability. Next steps push into shared goals, small accountability groups, and lightweight social features that make it harder to quietly quit on yourself.

  • Clear, low-friction goal setup with meaningful point values.
  • Reward store that matches real temptations instead of abstract badges.
  • Space for collaborative goals and small circles who keep each other honest.

Behaviour · Rewards

Reward Store

Behaviour design · Accountability

A simple store that holds your indulgences: gaming sessions, meals out, nights away. Each one has a point cost you set in advance.

When you “buy” a reward, your points drop. The friction isn’t fake – it makes every indulgence feel properly earned.

How I Built It

Goalsreach isn’t just a concept – it’s a shipped product. I designed the system, built the iOS app, and handled deployment, analytics, and iteration myself.

Engineering

iOS Development · Swift / SwiftUI

End-to-end build in Swift / SwiftUI with a focus on smooth navigation, predictable state, and clean architecture.

  • Feature work from sketch → prototype → shipped build.
  • Modular views and view models for maintainability.
  • Thoughtful use of animations and haptics to guide behaviour.
Product design

Flows, visuals, and behaviour

Designed to feel like a calm companion rather than a gamified, shouty productivity app.

  • Information architecture centred on the daily decision: “Earn or spend?”.
  • Figma prototypes for key flows and edge cases.
  • Iteration based on how real users actually try to bend the rules.
Infrastructure

Data, sync & future collaboration

Building the foundations for shared goals and small accountability groups.

  • Firebase-backed data model for goals, points, rewards, and streaks.
  • Structure designed for future real-time sync without noisy UI.
  • Clean separation of local state from network concerns.
